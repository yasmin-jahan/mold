Less-CommonlyRecognized Symptoms of Mold Illness

While some symptoms of mold illness are well-known, others can be less readily associated with environmental triggers. These may include:

Importance of Comprehensive Evaluation:

These symptoms can be caused by various underlying conditions. A comprehensive evaluation that considers your medical history, current symptoms andpotential environmental triggers is crucial for an accurate diagnosis and effective treatment plan.

Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome (POTS):

Characterized by lightheadedness or dizziness upon standing.

Gastroparesis:

Delayed stomach emptying, leading to nausea and vomiting.

Histamine Intolerance:

Reactions to histamine, a compound released by the body during inflammation.

Balance and Coordination Issues:

Difficulty maintaining balance or experiencing clumsiness.

Peripheral Neuropathy:

Numbness, tingling, or pain in the hands and feet.

Ear, Nose, and Throat (ENT) Problems:

Persistent sinus congestion, headaches, or sore throat.

Cardiac and Pulmonary Hypertension:

High blood pressure in either the heart or lungs.

Mold Exposure and its Potential Impact on the Brain

Exposure to water-damaged environments can trigger an inflammatory response the brain. This may lead to the rapid onset of symptoms like brain fog, mental fatigue, anxiety, and heart palpitations.

Chronic exposure can further disrupt the body’s delicate chemical balance, impacting various systems. Vision function may be affected, motivation can decrease, and weight gain may occur. In some cases, studies using advanced brain imaging techniques like NeuroQuant (Dr. Ross) have shown potential damage to brain structures.

Explore the CIRS Connection:

Water damage and microbial growth can trigger CIRS. We offer a patient-centered approach to identify the root cause and develop a personalized treatment plan.

It’s important to note:

  • Not everyone exposed to mold will experience these effects.
  • The severity of symptoms can vary greatly depending on individual factors.

Seeking Expert Guidance:

If you suspect mold exposure may be impacting your health, it’s crucial to consult a healthcare professional experienced in environmental health.
